Cleanthony Early had 24 points and 10 rebounds, helping No. 10 Wichita State extend the best start in team history with an 81-70 win over Davidson on Sunday.

Ron Baker scored 15 points for the host Shockers (13-0). Darius Carter added 13, and Tekele Cotton had 10.

Tyler Kalinoski had 17 points for Davidson (4-9), which trailed by only 4 at halftime.

Those guys arent going to quit; youve got to beat them, Wichita State Coach Gregg Marshall said of the Wildcats. That win will look a lot better later this season.

A key difference in the game was at the free-throw line. Wichita State was 28 of 41 from the line; Davidson was only 11 of 16. Three Wildcats fouled out.

OREGON 97, MORGAN STATE 76 Joseph Young scored 24 points as No. 12 Oregon remained unbeaten with a home victory over Morgan State.

Richard Amardi had 16 points in his first start of the season for the Ducks (12-0), who are off to their best start since going 13-0 in 2006-7. Justin Black scored 22 for the Bears (3-9).

Oregon, which entered the game as the highest-scoring team in N.C.A.A. Division I, increased its average to 90.8 points a game. The Ducks were 7 for 15 from 3-point range and shot 54 percent from the field over all.

FLORIDA 76, SAVANNAH STATE 34 Pat Young scored 15 points, becoming the 50th Florida player to reach 1,000 in his career, and the No. 13 Gators (10-2) routed Savannah State (2-11) for their 22nd consecutive home win.

Young, who reached the milestone on a putback with 7 minutes 15 seconds left, was one of four scorers in double figures for Florida.

NOTRE DAME 87, CANISIUS 81 Eric Atkins had a career-high 30 points to lead host Notre Dame (9-4) past Canisius (8-5) in overtime.

The Fighting Irish were without their leading scorer, the senior Jerian Grant, because of an academic violation. Atkins helped fill the void, shooting 10 of 14 from the field, including 6 of 8 from 3-point range.

Notre Dames Garrick Sherman converted a 3-point play and a putback late in overtime, and Atkins made his career-best sixth 3-pointer with 12 seconds remaining to seal the win.

I was just looking to make a play, Atkins said. Im not somebody who shows too much emotion. But I got a little hyped after that 3. It felt good to go in.

UCONN 67, CINCINNATI 34 Breanna Stewart had 12 points and 12 rebounds to lead No. 1 Connecticut past Cincinnati in the teams first matchup as members of the American Athletic Conference.

Moriah Jefferson and Bria Hartley each added 12 points for the Huskies (13-0, 1-0). The Bearcats (7-5, 0-1), winless in nine meetings against the Huskies, were led by Jeanise Randolph with 15 points and 10 rebounds.

Stewart was only 4 of 15 from the field, but she had four steals and two blocks in addition to her double-digit rebounding total.

Obviously if your shots not falling, you can still impact the game in different ways, Stewart said.

NOTRE DAME 70, OREGON ST. 58 Kayla McBride scored 20 points, Natalie Achonwa added 16 points and 8 rebounds, and No. 2 Notre Dame (11-0) won at Oregon State (8-4).

Jamie Weisner scored 18 points to lead Oregon State. Ruth Hamblin had nine blocks, tying a Beavers record.

Its a huge win for us, Notre Dame Coach Muffet McGraw said. Its great to be behind and have a close game at the end and see how we respond.

TENNESSEE 110, LIPSCOMB 42 Isabelle Harrison scored 26 points and grabbed 15 rebounds in only 18 minutes, leading No. 5 Tennessee (11-1) past Lipscomb (3-8) at home.

KENTUCKY 109, GRAMBLING 46 Bria Goss scored a career-high 23 points and had eight rebounds as No. 6 Kentucky (12-1) routed Grambling (3-8) at home.

LOUISVILLE 71, S.M.U. 51 Shoni Schimmel scored 18 points, Sara Hammond had 14 points and 10 rebounds, and No. 7 Louisville (13-1, 1-0 A.A.C.) beat visiting Southern Methodist (9-3, 0-1).

MARYLAND 103, CHARLESTON 51 Alyssa Thomas scored a season-high 28 points and had 10 rebounds, giving her 1,000 for her career, as No. 8 Maryland (12-1) coasted past College of Charleston (7-5) in the Terrapin Classic championship game in College Park, Md.
